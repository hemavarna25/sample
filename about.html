About Food Waste Management

Food waste management is the process of reducing, recovering, and recycling food waste to minimize its environmental impact. It involves various strategies such as:

  • Source reduction: Encouraging consumers and businesses to reduce food waste at the source by buying only what they need and using leftovers effectively.
  • Donation: Redistributing surplus food to those in need through food banks, shelters, and charitable organizations.
  • Composting: Recycling food scraps and organic waste to produce nutrient-rich compost for soil enrichment.
  • Energy recovery: Converting food waste into biogas or biofuel through anaerobic digestion or other technologies for renewable energy production.

Efficient food waste management helps conserve resources, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, alleviate hunger, and promote sustainability.
